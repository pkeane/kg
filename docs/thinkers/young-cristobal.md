# Cristobal Young

Economic sociologist, associate professor of sociology at Cornell University, previously at Stanford. *The Myth of Millionaire Tax Flight: How Place Still Matters for the Rich* (2017) tests the most common argument deployed against higher state and national taxes on the wealthy — that the rich will simply relocate to avoid them — against thirteen years of United States income-tax records covering filers reporting at least one million dollars. Young finds that millionaires migrate at a lower annual rate (about 2.4 percent) than the general population (about 2.9 percent), and that only a small fraction of the moves that occur are toward lower-tax states. He concludes that tax-induced flight of the wealthy is far smaller than commonly assumed, because location is bound up with the social and business networks through which fortunes are made and held. The finding is frequently cited against the claim that taxing the wealthy is self-defeating. See [[how-to-tax-the-wealthy]].
