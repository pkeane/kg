# Peter Kropotkin

Russian geographer, naturalist, and the principal theorist of anarchist communism. Born into the Moscow aristocracy — his father held the title of prince — Kropotkin served as a page at the imperial court and then as an army officer in Siberia, where five years of geographical fieldwork shaped both his scientific reputation and his political thinking. He renounced his title, joined the [[anarchism|anarchist]] wing of the First International, and was arrested in 1874; he escaped from prison in 1876 and spent the next four decades in exile in Western Europe — Switzerland, France (where he was again imprisoned), and finally England.

Kropotkin's anarchist communism was set against both Proudhon's mutualism and Bakunin's collectivism, each of which retained some link between a worker's contribution and reward. In *The Conquest of Bread* (1892) he argued instead for distribution according to need, with the wage system, money, and private property in the means of production abolished outright; he held that the productive capacity already existing in industrial society was sufficient to guarantee well-being to all, and that scarcity was a function of social organization rather than of nature. *Fields, Factories and Workshops* (1899) developed the practical side of this: a decentralized integration of agriculture and industry, and of manual and mental labor.

*Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution* (1902) is his most widely read book. Written against the "gladiatorial" reading of [[darwin-charles|Darwin]] associated with Thomas Huxley and the Social Darwinists, it drew on Kropotkin's Siberian observations to argue that cooperation within a species is itself a factor in evolution and a condition of survival, as significant as competition. The book did not deny natural selection; it disputed the claim that selection licensed a politics of struggle, and offered mutual aid as both a biological fact and a basis for social organization.

Kropotkin returned to Russia after the February Revolution of 1917. He supported the revolution but condemned the Bolshevik seizure of power and the centralizing, authoritarian state that followed. He died near Moscow in 1921; his funeral drew tens of thousands and was the last large anarchist demonstration permitted in Soviet Russia.

## Key ideas

- Anarchist communism: distribution according to need, abolition of the wage system
- Mutual aid as a factor in evolution and a basis for social organization
- The critique of Social Darwinism
- Decentralized integration of agriculture and industry, manual and mental labor
- Anarchism as continuous with the natural and social sciences

## Key works
