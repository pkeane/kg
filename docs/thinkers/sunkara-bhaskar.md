# Bhaskar Sunkara

Bhaskar Sunkara (b. 1989) is an American writer, editor, and publisher, the founder of the socialist magazine *Jacobin* and, since 2021, president of *The Nation*. He is among the most visible figures of the publishing apparatus that accompanied the revival of the American socialist Left after 2008.

Sunkara founded *Jacobin* in 2010, while an undergraduate, as a print and online quarterly of socialist politics and culture. Over the following decade it grew into the most widely circulated journal of the American Left, combining Marxist analysis with accessible polemic and design, and it became closely associated with the post-2016 expansion of the [[democratic-socialists-of-america|Democratic Socialists of America]] and the campaigns of Bernie [[sanders-bernie|Sanders]]. He later launched the British sister publication *Tribune* (reviving an older Labour-left title) and the academic-adjacent journal *Catalyst*.

His book *The Socialist Manifesto: The Case for Radical Politics in an Era of Extreme Inequality* (2019) sets out an argument for democratic socialism aimed at a general readership, surveying the history of socialist movements and making the case for a renewed social-democratic-to-socialist program achieved through mass politics and electoral organization. The position is recognizably in the lineage of Michael [[harrington-michael|Harrington]]'s "left wing of the possible," updated for a generation radicalized by the 2008 financial crisis.

## Key ideas

- *Jacobin* as a project to build a popular, readable socialist publication and political culture
- A democratic road to socialism through mass organization and electoral politics
- Class politics and redistribution over identity-based or purely cultural framings of the Left
- Socialist publishing as movement infrastructure
