# Paul Otlet

Belgian lawyer, bibliographer, and internationalist (23 August 1868, Brussels – 10 December 1944, Brussels), often called the father of documentation, or information science. With Henri La Fontaine — the jurist who won the 1913 Nobel Peace Prize — he founded the Institut International de Bibliographie in 1895 and set out to compile a complete record of everything ever published.

The result was the Répertoire Bibliographique Universel, a card catalog that grew to more than fifteen million standardized index cards, queryable by mail. To organize it Otlet and La Fontaine adapted Melvil Dewey's scheme into the Universal Decimal Classification (UDC), a faceted system that allowed entries to be combined and cross-related rather than filed under a single heading. Otlet argued for "documentation" as a discipline broader than bibliography: the analysis of recorded knowledge into discrete facts that could be extracted from books, recombined, and linked.

He housed the project in the Mundaneum (also called the Palais Mondial), an institution in Brussels intended as a repository of universal knowledge and a node in a planned global network of information centers — part of a wider internationalist program he shared with La Fontaine. In *Traité de documentation* (1934) and *Monde* (1935) Otlet described a future workstation, sometimes rendered as the "Mondotheque," at which a person could retrieve linked documents, images, and sound at a distance over a telecommunications network — a vision later read as an anticipation of hypertext and the web. The Mundaneum lost government support, and much of its collection was scattered or destroyed during the German occupation in the Second World War. Otlet's work was largely forgotten until the bibliographer W. Boyd Rayward recovered it from the 1970s onward; the surviving archive is now a museum in Mons, Belgium.

## Key ideas

- **Documentation**: the systematic analysis, classification, and linking of recorded knowledge, conceived more broadly than bibliography
- The **Universal Decimal Classification (UDC)**, a faceted scheme allowing combinable, cross-related subject codes
- A **universal bibliographic repository** (the Répertoire) as collective memory
- The **Mondotheque**: a networked workstation for remote retrieval of linked documents, anticipating hypertext

## Key works

- *Traité de documentation: le livre sur le livre* (1934)
- *Monde: essai d'universalisme* (1935)
