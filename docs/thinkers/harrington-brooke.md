# Brooke Harrington

American economic sociologist (b. 1968), a professor of sociology at Dartmouth College, previously at the Copenhagen Business School. To study how large fortunes are protected from taxation she trained and qualified as a wealth manager, then interviewed practitioners in financial centers and offshore jurisdictions across several continents. *Capital without Borders: Wealth Managers and the One Percent* (2016) is the resulting ethnography of the profession, documenting the trusts, shell companies, foundations, and offshore structures through which the very wealthy place assets beyond the reach of national tax and regulatory authorities, and arguing that this professional apparatus is a significant and understudied driver of inequality. *Offshore: Stealth Wealth and the New Colonialism* (2024) extends the account to the jurisdictions that host such wealth.
