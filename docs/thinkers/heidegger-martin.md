# Martin Heidegger

German philosopher whose *Sein und Zeit* (*Being and Time*, 1927) reframed twentieth-century continental philosophy around the question of being (*die Seinsfrage*) and the structure of human existence (*Dasein*). Trained under [[husserl-edmund|Edmund Husserl]] at Freiburg and named his successor in the chair of philosophy, Heidegger turned phenomenology away from the analysis of consciousness toward the analysis of being-in-the-world.

*Being and Time* argues that the philosophical tradition since Plato has forgotten the question of what it means *to be*, displacing it with questions about beings (objects, substances, properties). Heidegger proposes to recover the question by analyzing the one being for whom being is itself an issue: *Dasein*, the entity each of us is. *Dasein* is characterized by thrownness (*Geworfenheit*) — we find ourselves already in a world, a language, a history, a body, a set of practical concerns we did not choose — and by projection (*Entwurf*) toward possibilities. Theoretical knowing is a derivative mode of a more primary practical engagement; the world is encountered first as a network of equipment ready-to-hand (*Zuhanden*) and only secondarily as objects present-at-hand (*Vorhanden*) for inspection. Anxiety (*Angst*) discloses the groundlessness of *Dasein*'s existence; being-toward-death individualizes it.

Heidegger's later work, after the so-called *Kehre* ("turn") of the 1930s, moved away from the systematic analytic of *Being and Time* toward meditative readings of Hölderlin, the pre-Socratics, and the history of metaphysics, and toward a critique of modern technology ("The Question Concerning Technology," 1954) as a mode of revealing that reduces everything to "standing-reserve" (*Bestand*).

Heidegger joined the Nazi Party in May 1933 and served as rector of Freiburg until April 1934, when he resigned. He remained a Party member until 1945, never publicly repudiated the affiliation, and the publication of the *Schwarze Hefte* ("Black Notebooks") from 2014 onward made the antisemitic content of his private thought more difficult to bracket. The relationship between the philosophy and the politics remains a live scholarly question.

His influence runs through [[sartre-jean-paul|Sartre]] and [[de-beauvoir-simone|Beauvoir]]'s French existentialism, [[merleau-ponty-maurice|Merleau-Ponty]]'s phenomenology of perception, Gadamer's philosophical hermeneutics, Arendt's political theory, Derrida's deconstruction, and — by way of [[dreyfus-hubert|Hubert Dreyfus]] — the embodied-cognition critique of artificial intelligence. See [[situated-knowledge]] for the broader topic.

## Key ideas

- Being-in-the-world (*In-der-Welt-sein*) as the basic structure of *Dasein*
- Thrownness (*Geworfenheit*) and projection (*Entwurf*)
- Ready-to-hand versus present-at-hand
- Anxiety and being-toward-death
- The forgetting of being (*Seinsvergessenheit*)
- Technology as enframing (*Gestell*); standing-reserve (*Bestand*)

## Key works

- *Being and Time* (*Sein und Zeit*, 1927)
- "What Is Metaphysics?" (1929)
- *Introduction to Metaphysics* (lectures 1935, published 1953)
- "The Origin of the Work of Art" (1936)
- "Letter on Humanism" (1947)
- "The Question Concerning Technology" (1954)
- *Schwarze Hefte* (Black Notebooks, published 2014–)
