# Isaac William Martin

American sociologist, the Jeri-Ann and Gary E. Jacobs Professor of Social Science in Urban Studies and Planning at the University of California, San Diego. His research concerns the politics of taxation and property. *The Permanent Tax Revolt: How the Property Tax Transformed American Politics* (2008) traces the property-tax rebellions of the 1970s — notably California's Proposition 13 — and their lasting effect on American politics. *Rich People's Movements: Grassroots Campaigns to Untax the One Percent* (2013) is a history of organized mobilization on behalf of the wealthy against progressive taxation since the 1913 federal income tax, identifying recurring waves of protest that adopted the tactics of populist, progressive, and later social movements. The work documents that resistance to taxing the rich has frequently been deliberately organized rather than spontaneous. See [[how-to-tax-the-wealthy]].
