# Jeffrey A. Winters

American political scientist at Northwestern University, where he founded and directs the Equality Development and Globalization Studies program. His comparative study *Oligarchy* (2011) defines oligarchs as actors who command concentrated material resources they can deploy to defend their wealth, and distinguishes political regimes by how that wealth defense is organized. In what he calls the "civil oligarchy" of the contemporary United States, the wealthy no longer defend their property by force and instead concentrate their political resources on resisting taxation, employing an "income defense industry" of lawyers, accountants, and lobbyists to reduce their liabilities. Winters argues that oligarchy and democracy can coexist — formal political equality alongside extreme material inequality. His earlier *Power in Motion* (1996) analyzed capital mobility and the Indonesian state. See [[how-to-tax-the-wealthy]].
