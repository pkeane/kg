# Larry Bartels

American political scientist (b. 1956), the May Werthan Shayne Chair in Public Policy and Social Science at Vanderbilt University and previously founding director of the Center for the Study of Democratic Politics at Princeton. *Unequal Democracy: The Political Economy of the New Gilded Age* (2008; second edition 2016) is his account of how United States economic policy and political representation favor the affluent. Using roll-call votes and constituency data, Bartels finds that senators are far more responsive to the preferences of high-income constituents than to middle-income ones, and show no measurable response to the views of constituents in the bottom third of the income distribution.

The book also documents a partisan asymmetry — income inequality has tended to widen under Republican administrations and narrow under Democratic ones — and argues that the post-1970s rise in inequality reflects policy choices rather than impersonal economic forces. With Christopher Achen he wrote *Democracy for Realists* (2016), a critique of the "folk theory" that elections translate citizen preferences into policy. He received the National Medal of Science in 2025. See [[how-to-tax-the-wealthy]].
