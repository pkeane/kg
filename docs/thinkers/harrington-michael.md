# Michael Harrington

Edward Michael Harrington Jr. (1928–1989) was an American democratic socialist, writer, and political organizer, best known as the founder of the [[democratic-socialists-of-america|Democratic Socialists of America]] and as the author of *The Other America: Poverty in the United States* (1962). Raised Catholic in St. Louis and briefly active in Dorothy Day's Catholic Worker movement, he became the leading public exponent of democratic socialism in the United States in the second half of the twentieth century.

*The Other America* argued that some 40 to 50 million Americans lived in an "invisible" poverty hidden by postwar affluence — the elderly, agricultural and unskilled workers, racial minorities, residents of depressed regions. The book's reception is often credited with helping prompt the antipoverty initiatives of the Kennedy and Johnson administrations; it was read inside the administration and cited in the framing of the War on Poverty, though the precise extent of its influence is debated. It remains Harrington's most widely read work.

Harrington came out of the Socialist Party of America and its youth organizations, working with the labor and civil-rights organizer Bayard Rustin and the older socialist leader Norman Thomas. After the Socialist Party split in 1972–73, he founded the Democratic Socialist Organizing Committee (1973), which in 1982 merged with the New American Movement to form DSA. His strategic position — that socialists should work as the "left wing of the possible," operating inside the Democratic Party and the labor movement rather than as a separate party — shaped the American democratic-socialist tradition that later figures such as Bernie [[sanders-bernie|Sanders]] and the editors of *Jacobin* inherited and contested.

## Key ideas

- "Invisible" poverty amid postwar affluence (*The Other America*)
- Democratic socialism as the "left wing of the possible": working within the Democratic Party and labor movement
- The realignment strategy later institutionalized in DSA
- Socialism as the extension of democracy into economic life

## Key works

- *The Other America: Poverty in the United States* (1962)
- *Socialism* (1972)
- *The Twilight of Capitalism* (1976)
- *The Politics at God's Funeral* (1983)
- *Socialism: Past and Future* (1989)
