Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez

1989 – ? · American
#democratic-socialism#dsa#american-left#electoral-politics#green-new-deal

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (b. 1989, the Bronx, New York), often referred to by the initials "AOC," is an American politician and member of the United States House of Representatives, representing New York's 14th congressional district since 2019. A member of the Democratic Socialists of America, she became the most prominent figure of the post-2016 American socialist Left after an upset primary victory.

In the 2018 Democratic primary she defeated Joseph Crowley, a ten-term incumbent and the fourth-ranking House Democrat widely discussed as a potential Speaker, in one of the more consequential primary upsets in recent congressional history. She had previously worked on Bernie Sanders's 2016 presidential campaign. In office she became closely associated with the Green New Deal, a non-binding resolution she introduced with Senator Edward Markey in 2019 calling for the decarbonization of the U.S. economy paired with a federal jobs and social-provision program. She is identified with the informal group of progressive House members known as "the Squad."

Her relationship to DSA and to the wider Left has at times been a subject of friction — over endorsements, over votes, and over the strategic question of how far an elected socialist should accommodate the institutional Democratic Party — making her a recurring case in debates about whether the movement's electoral gains translate into legislative power, the question taken up in Democratic Socialism and the Problem of Scale.

Key ideas

  • The Green New Deal: climate decarbonization joined to a federal jobs guarantee and social provision
  • Insurgent primary challenges to establishment Democrats as a route to power
  • Small-donor fundraising and grassroots field organizing
  • Higher marginal taxation of top incomes (the proposed top rate that drew national attention in 2019)

Key works
