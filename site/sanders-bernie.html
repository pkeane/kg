Bernie Sanders

1941 – ? · American
#democratic-socialism#american-left#electoral-politics#labor#inequality

Bernard Sanders (b. 1941, Brooklyn, New York) is an American politician, United States Senator from Vermont since 2007, and the figure most responsible for moving the language of democratic socialism into the mainstream of American electoral politics in the twenty-first century. He sits in the Senate as an independent but caucuses with the Democratic Party, and sought the Democratic presidential nomination in 2016 and 2020.

Sanders entered politics through the small Liberty Union Party in Vermont in the early 1970s, then won election as mayor of Burlington in 1981 by ten votes. He served four terms before entering the U.S. House of Representatives in 1991 as an independent and the Senate in 2007. Across this career his program remained consistent: a higher minimum wage, single-payer health care ("Medicare for All"), expanded public provision, higher taxation of large fortunes and corporations, and a critique of what he calls the political power of "the billionaire class." He has repeatedly cited Eugene Debs as a political hero.

His 2016 primary campaign against Hillary Clinton, unexpectedly competitive, brought the term "democratic socialist" into wide use and coincided with the tenfold expansion of the Democratic Socialists of America, though Sanders himself is not a DSA member. He defines his socialism largely in terms of the New Deal and Nordic social democracy rather than public ownership of the means of production. His campaigns and Senate office served as a training ground and reference point for a younger cohort of left politicians, including Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, who worked on his 2016 campaign, and Zohran Mamdani.

Key ideas

  • Democratic socialism framed as the New Deal extended and Nordic-style social democracy
  • "Medicare for All"; a Green New Deal; tuition-free public higher education
  • The "billionaire class" and the corrupting role of concentrated wealth in politics
  • Mass small-donor fundraising as an alternative to corporate and large-donor money

  • Our Revolution: A Future to Believe In (2016)
