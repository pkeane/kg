Democratic Socialists of America (DSA)

20th–21st century (1982–present) · American
#democratic-socialism#socialism#american-left#dsa#electoral-politics#labor

The Democratic Socialists of America is the largest socialist organization in the United States. It was formed in 1982 by the merger of the Democratic Socialist Organizing Committee (DSOC) and the New American Movement (NAM). DSOC had been founded in 1973 by Michael Harrington — author of The Other America (1962) — after his break from the Socialist Party of America; NAM was a New Left–rooted group of 1970s activists. DSA is a membership organization and political pressure group rather than a political party: its members generally run for office and organize on the Democratic Party ballot line rather than as a third party.

DSA situates itself in the democratic-socialist tradition descending from Eduard Bernstein's evolutionary socialism and the social-democratic parties of Western Europe, and in the American line from Eugene Debs and Norman Thomas through Harrington. Harrington's "realignment" strategy — pushing the Democratic Party leftward rather than building an independent socialist party — has remained the organization's dominant orientation, though it is internally contested. DSA is a "big tent" containing social democrats (oriented toward Nordic-style welfare capitalism), democratic socialists favoring public ownership of major sectors, and Marxist and other caucuses (among them Bread & Roses and the Libertarian Socialist Caucus).

The organization was small for most of its history — roughly 6,000–8,000 members — and grew sharply after Bernie Sanders's 2016 presidential primary campaign and Donald Trump's election, reaching the tens of thousands and exceeding 90,000 members by around 2021 (DSA's own reporting). Its best-known elected figures are Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and Rashida Tlaib, both elected to Congress in 2018, and Zohran Mamdani, elected mayor of New York City in 2025. Its principal policy commitments include Medicare for All, a Green New Deal, labor and tenant organizing, and a broadly redistributive program. The question of whether the organization's city-level electoral successes can be reproduced at state and national scale is taken up in Democratic Socialism and the Problem of Scale.

Key facts

  • Founded 1982 by the merger of DSOC (Harrington) and NAM
  • A multi-tendency membership organization, not a party; operates largely on the Democratic ballot line
  • "Realignment" strategy: transform the Democratic Party from within
  • Tenfold-plus membership growth after the 2016 Sanders campaign
  • Associated elected officials: Ocasio-Cortez, Tlaib (Congress, 2018); Mamdani (NYC mayor, 2025)

Secondary sources

  • Maurice Isserman, The Other American: The Life of Michael Harrington (2000) — biography of DSA's founding figure.
  • Michael Harrington, Socialism: Past and Future (1989) — Harrington's mature statement of the democratic-socialist program.
  • Bhaskar Sunkara, The Socialist Manifesto (2019) — a contemporary case for democratic socialism from the founder of Jacobin, aligned with the post-2016 DSA milieu.
