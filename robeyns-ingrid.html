Ingrid Robeyns

1972 – ? · Belgian-Dutch
#philosophy#ethics#inequality#wealth#distributive-justice

Belgian-Dutch philosopher and economist (b. 1972, Leuven), Chair in the Ethics of Institutions at Utrecht University. She took a doctorate in economics at Cambridge in 2003 and is a leading developer of the capability approach to justice associated with Amartya Sen, on which she wrote Wellbeing, Freedom and Social Justice (2017).

Limitarianism: The Case Against Extreme Wealth (Astra House / Allen Lane, 2024) argues that there is an upper limit to how much wealth it is morally permissible for one person to hold, and that great fortunes should be capped — by taxation and other means — well below the levels now common. Part of the argument is a direct rebuttal of the claim that the very rich deserve their wealth: Robeyns contends that large fortunes are produced by luck, inheritance, market structure, and the contributions of others far more than by individual merit, so the intuition that taxing them takes what their holders earned does not hold. She pairs this desert argument with claims that concentrated wealth erodes democracy, entrenches poverty, and drives ecological harm. The book grew out of her "Fair Limits" research project at Utrecht. See How to Tax the Wealthy.

Key ideas

  • Limitarianism — a moral and political ceiling on permissible personal wealth
  • The rebuttal of desert: great fortunes are not earned in proportion to merit
  • Concentrated wealth as a threat to democracy and ecological stability
